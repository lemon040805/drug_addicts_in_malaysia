About 284 million people used drugs in 2020 — roughly 1 in 18 adults — and this is about a 26% increase since 2010. The most commonly used drug is cannabis (around 209 million people, about 4% of adults), followed by amphetamines (about 34 million people, roughly 0.7% of the global population).

Drug use is linked to around 5% of substance-related deaths and about 9% of the overall health burden (DALYs) from substance use.

During COVID-19 lockdowns, teen drug use fell in many places, likely as schools and social activities closed.

National data (NHMS 2019) indicate marijuana is the most used illicit drug, followed by kratom. Among adolescents aged 13–18 in 2020, amphetamine-type stimulants (ATS) were most common, then marijuana and opiates (AADK).

How Is The Situation Trending?

During 2020 – 2021 detections dipped nationwide as movement controls disrupted outreach, testing, court processing, and voluntary presentations; the rate fell from 436 per 100,000 in 2019 to 394 in 2020 and 377 in 2021 (about a −13.5% slide from 2019).

From 2022 onward detections rose as services reopened and enforcement normalised: rates rebounded to 420 (2022), returned to 436 (2023), and reached a new high of 498 (2024; +14.2% year-on-year). In counts, cases grew from 123,139 (2021) to 169,691 (2024), roughly +37.8% overall.

Source: Statistics Of Drug And Substance Abuse And Addiction – Agensi Antidadah Kebangsaan (AADK)
Where Is it happening most?

Persistent hotspots
Northern and northeast Peninsular states — Kelantan, Terengganu, Perlis and Kedah — tend to show the heaviest shading across years, forming a contiguous high-detection corridor.

Lower baseline
East Malaysia (Sabah, Sarawak) and the Klang Valley core (Selangor) are generally lighter on the map relative to northern/eastern states.

Post-pandemic rebound
Many states darken again from 2022 as outreach resumes and enforcement normalises after COVID-19 disruptions.

Small-area volatility
Federal territories — especially Putrajaya and Labuan — can shift shading quickly year to year because small populations amplify changes in detections.

What Is Being Used?
Drug categories
  • ATS (Amphetamine-Type Stimulants):
    • Methamphetamine (Crystalline): Syabu, Ice, Batu
    • Methamphetamine (Tablet): Pil Kuda, YABA, YAMA, “Bomb”
    • Other ATS: Ecstasy, Amphetamine
  • Opiates: Opium, Heroin, Morphine, Codeine
  • Cannabis: Ganja, Hashish, Marijuana
  • Others: Kratom (Ketum), Cocaine, Ketamine, Depressants, Dissociatives, Hallucinogens, Inhalants, etc.
  • Psychotropic Pills: Benzodiazepines, Eramin 5, APO 5, Dormicum

ATS dominates across all years, widening its lead again after 2022. This likely reflects both continued demand for stimulants and active detection in this category.

Opiates trend downward from earlier years, with a modest rebound most recently. The category remains clearly below ATS by the end of the period.

Cannabis is rising from a low baseline, especially post-pandemic, but still accounts for a smaller share of detections than ATS or opiates.

Psychotropic pills & Others remain minor in level yet show steady growth after 2022 — this may be influenced by shifts in policing focus, testing, or reporting.

Source: Statistics Of Drug And Substance Abuse And Addicts By Drug Category – Agensi Antidadah Kebangsaan (AADK)
Who is most affected? — By Age Group

Children — minimal impact. Detections are rare and remain consistently low compared with all other groups.

Youth trending lower. After a peak before the pandemic, detections eased and now sit well below the working-age groups.

Young adults remain elevated. Long the leading group, detections recovered quickly after pandemic disruptions and remain high.

Middle-aged adults now highest. A steady climb has pushed this group above young adults in the latest year, reflecting an ageing cohort and longer duration of use.

Older adults rising from a low base. Detections are growing faster than other groups yet still remain below the working-age peak.

Which Sex Is Most Affected?

Sex disparity persists Men account for the vast majority of detections (order-of-magnitude higher than women). Both sexes dipped in 2020–2021 and reached new highs as services and enforcement normalised from 2022.

What happens to detected cases?

Rising share of repeat cases
The balance shifts over the years from mostly first-time detections toward a much larger share of repeat detections.

Pandemic pivot
During COVID-19 disruptions, first-time detections fell more sharply while repeat detections made up a bigger slice of the total.

Post-2022 recovery
As services and enforcement return to normal, both first-time and repeat detections climb, with repeat growing faster and narrowing the gap.

Sources: Statistics Of Drug And Substance Abusers And Addicts By Case Status – Agensi Antidadah Kebangsaan (AADK)

What to expect in rehabilitation (Malaysia-specific)
Voluntary admission (PUSPEN / Cure & Care Clinics)
Intake, assessment, detox, counselling, medication-assisted treatment (for opiates), vocational training, and aftercare. Typical programme windows are ~6–24 months and tailored to individual needs. Voluntary clients at clinics are not subjected to legal action for presenting themselves.

Community-based models (Cure & Care Clinics/outreach) versus institution-based treatment (PUSPEN). The right option depends on assessment, substance type, and available support.
